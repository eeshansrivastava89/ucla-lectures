You spent five weeks building a product with free tools on your laptop. You deployed a churn prediction API, tracked experiments, containerized the service, added monitoring and CI/CD, and wrapped it all with an LLM-powered interface. Every tool you touched was open-source and ran on your machine with zero cloud spend.

This page is the bridge from that experience to the industry. Every tool you used has an enterprise counterpart. FastAPI becomes an API gateway with load balancing and auth. Docker Compose becomes Kubernetes. Evidently becomes Arize. The concepts are identical — drift is drift whether you detect it with Evidently or Fiddler. The infrastructure changes. The architecture does not.

The table below maps every FOSS tool to its enterprise counterpart, lecture by lecture. The last section shows what changes at each level of scale: availability, observability, security, compliance, and cost. These are the dimensions that separate a proof of concept from a production system serving millions of users.

You walk away knowing not just how to build an ML product, but exactly what changes when the business says "we need this at scale."
