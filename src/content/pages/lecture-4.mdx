A model that worked at deployment can silently degrade. Data changes shape, pipelines break without warning, and biases that were invisible in training show up once real people are affected. This lecture adds the safety layer: tests, monitoring, and CI/CD.

We draw a clear line between three problems that often get lumped together. Data quality asks whether the incoming data is well-formed — schema violations, missing fields, and type errors are not drift, they are garbage in. Drift asks whether the distribution has changed — data drift means the inputs shifted, concept drift means the relationship between inputs and outputs shifted, and they require different responses. Feedback loops ask whether our predictions changed the world — if we recommend products and users click them, are we learning what users want, or are we learning what we recommended? This is the hardest problem to detect because nothing is obviously broken.

We also address bias auditing and A/B testing. Fairness is not a nice-to-have — MSBA students will be the ones asked "is this model fair?" within their first month on the job. A/B testing is how you answer "is the new model actually better?" with evidence, not intuition.
