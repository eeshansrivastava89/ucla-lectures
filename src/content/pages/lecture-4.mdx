A model that worked at deployment can silently degrade. Data changes shape, pipelines break without warning, and biases that were invisible in training show up once real people are affected. This lecture adds the safety layer.

Three problems that look like the same thing. <span class="accent">Data quality</span> asks whether the incoming data is well-formed — schema violations, missing fields, and type errors are not drift, they are garbage in. <span class="accent">Drift</span> asks whether the distribution has changed — data drift means the inputs shifted, concept drift means the relationship between inputs and outputs shifted. These require different responses. <span class="accent">Feedback loops</span> ask whether your predictions changed the world: if you recommend products and users click them, are you learning what users want, or are you learning what you recommended? This is the hardest problem to catch because nothing looks broken.

We also cover bias auditing and A/B testing. <span class="accent">Fairness is not optional</span> — MSBA graduates are often the first person asked "is this model fair?" in their organization. A/B testing is how you answer "is the new model actually better?" with evidence instead of instinct. By the end of this lecture your pipeline has tests, monitoring, and a CI workflow that runs on every push.
