When you train one model, you remember what you did. When you train fifty, you don't. This lecture introduces experiment tracking — the production habit that separates responsible ML from ad-hoc ML.

Students track experiments, compare models on multiple dimensions, and register the best one. They swap their hardcoded model for a *versioned, auditable* one. This is also where we introduce the idea that "best" is not just accuracy — latency, cost, and fairness all matter.
